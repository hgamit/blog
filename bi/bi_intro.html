Formulating The Core of The Analysis

January 5, 2020. 5 Minutes Read

References:
  • Business Intelligence and Data Warehousing
  • Dimensional Modelling

This blog post details format that needs to be considered while understanding and transforming data tables into business insights.

Formulating The Core Analysis:

  1. Establish the business user's requirements.
  2. Define the conceptual Model and Determine the grain of the data.
  3. Identify the Dimensions (Analyze by what?)
  4. Identify the Facts/Business Number (What Is most important subject measure? What could be Key Performance Indicators?)
  5. Finalizing and evaluating Key Performance Indicators and Keeping an eye on Signals.

1. Establish the business user's requirements. 

Usually, Business user's focus is on gaining knowledge through movements in the data.For example, Universities wants to improve student engagement. To define key performance indicators, Data Scientist plays very big role providing business insights to drive strategic decisions based on any given data. One should always ask following queries before preparing KPI building data pipeline.

  1. Who is the intended audience? E.g., Monitoring Administrator for Universities Data.
  2. What questions does this visualization answer? Number of Lab Users, Most Used Spaces, Term Seasonality, Unused areas, Peak Weeks throughout the semester/month/year etc.
  3. What data is represented in this analysis/visualization?  Most of the times, Date and Time, space, equipemnts, User Hits.
  4. How is the data analytically/visually encoded? predictions, Heatmap, Trend Line, Bar Chart, other ML/DL methods.
  5. Why do you like / dislike this analytics/visualization? Does it answer necessary question and is it easy for business user to identify.

2. Define the conceptual Model and Determine the grain of the data.

Conceptual Data Models: Conceptual Data Models are very important for helping the end-user to understand the data that is available and the basic relationships. This is specialty designed to be a ‘User-Friendly’ Model that shows the structure of the data that is available to support Analytics, Reports and BI requirements. It can be described as follows: Passengers make Reservations for Scheduled Flights operated by Airlines. They take Flights and make Payments which generate Revenue

Grain Evaluation: Lowest level at which business numbers are measured. What is lowest level of information is stored in data table?. For better Reporting and Analytics - Keep lowest possible grain as it can be to allow appropriate drilling. For E.g. In Date Time Field, Year-->Semester-->Quarter-->Month-->Week-->Day-->Hour.

3. Identify the Dimensions (Analyze by what?)

Caonceptual data model for airlines will have Date, Flight Name, Seats, Prices, Locations, Reservations and Passengers. In this fields, text values such as Date, Flight Name, Locations can be used to describe seats, prices, reservations and passengers. So, the fields that describes this quantitative numbers are Dimensions. Dimensions are used for Slicing and Dicing, Filtering, Grouping, Labelling etc.

4. Identify the Facts/Business Number (What Is most important subject measure? What could be Key Performance Indicators?)

A Fact in business data tables provides quantitative information about business process. It is business measure on which Average, Totals, Percentages, KPIs, and Trend Figures are derived. In above airline example, Seats, Prices, Reservations and Passenger numbers could be used as Business Number to understand Airline Performance over a period of time.

5. Finalizing and evaluating Key Performance Indicators and Keeping an eye on Signals.

Once we settle down on Business Numbers and Dimensions explaining data tables. It could give several charts and plots to analyse data movements. It is very important to define KPIs and its thresholds on performance whether the data shows growth or not to take strategic decisions.
