Basics of Data

Dec 3, 2019. 5 Minutes Read

References:
  • What is Data?
  • NPTEL - Applied Time-Series Analysis

This blog post shows basics of data and interpreting it's context. Data is a collection of facts, such as numbers, categories, measurements, observations or even just descriptions of things.

Types of Data:

Data can be qualitative or quantitative.

  • Qualitative data is descriptive information (it describes something)
  • Quantitative data is numerical information (numbers)

For Example: What do we know about Arrow the Dog?

In Simple words: Discrete data is counted, Continuous data is measured

Qualitative Quantitative: Discrete Quantitative: Continuous
  • He is brown and black
  • He has long hair
  • He has lots of energy
  • He has 4 legs
  • He has 2 brothers
  • He weighs 25.5 kg
  • He is 565 mm tall
More Examples:
Qualitative Quantitative
  • Your friends' favorite holiday destination
  • The most common given names in your town
  • How people describe the smell of a new perfume
  • Height (Continuous)
  • Weight (Continuous)
  • Petals on a flower (Discrete)
  • Customers in a shop (Discrete)
